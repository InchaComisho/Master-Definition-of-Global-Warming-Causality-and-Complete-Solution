## 1. Purpose

This document provides a shared MRV framework for connecting Master’s definition of global warming causality and the complete solution to implementable business models.

The goal is to evaluate each business model not only by revenue or CO₂ reduction, but by how much it restores the following four planetary circulations:

```text
1. Water phase-transition circulation
2. Atmospheric circulation
3. Ocean circulation
4. Food and organic matter circulation
```

Each business model is therefore redefined as:

```text
Business model
=
Revenue activity
+
Circulation recovery mechanism
+
MRV target
+
Cooling Credit candidate
+
Civilization OS implementation unit
```

## 2. Common Evaluation Axes

| Circulation | What is evaluated | Representative indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Water phase-transition circulation | Water retention, evaporation, evapotranspiration, cloud/rain linkage | Soil moisture, evapotranspiration, land-surface temperature, infiltration rate |

| Food and organic matter circulation | Returning organic matter to soil instead of incineration | Humus, soil organic carbon, microbial diversity, avoided incineration |

## 4. Model 1: Urban Water-Cycle and Urban Cooling

### Overview

This model transforms cities from heat-storing asphalt and concrete structures into cooling systems that circulate water and vegetation.

### Circulation recovery pathway

```text
Rainwater storage and reclaimed water use
    ↓
Urban waterways, mist, greening, water-retentive pavements
    ↓
Lower surface temperature and WBGT
    ↓
Recovery of evaporation and evapotranspiration
    ↓
Support for water phase-transition and atmospheric circulation recovery
```

### Indicators

| Circulation | Indicators |
|---|---|
| Water phase-transition | Rainwater storage, reclaimed water use, evaporation/evapotranspiration |
| Atmospheric | WBGT reduction, urban temperature reduction, shade ratio, mist cooling |
| Ocean | Indirect: reduced urban runoff stress |
| Organic matter | Humification of leaves and pruned branches |

### Cooling Credit candidates

- Measured land-surface temperature reduction
- WBGT improvement
- Cooling electricity reduction
- Urban water-cycle recovery
- Greening and evapotranspiration recovery

## 5. Model 2: Food-Loss Humification and Organic Matter Circulation

This model converts food loss, food residues, and organic waste into humus through drying, grinding, sanitation, and microbial decomposition instead of incineration.

### Circulation recovery pathway

```text
Food-loss reduction
    ↓
Avoided incineration
    ↓
Humification and composting
    ↓
Soil microorganism recovery
    ↓
Higher soil water retention
    ↓
Evapotranspiration recovery
    ↓
Recovery of water phase-transition and organic matter circulation
```

### Indicators

| Circulation | Indicators |
|---|---|
| Water phase-transition | Soil moisture, evapotranspiration, land-surface temperature reduction |
| Atmospheric | Indirect: increased evapotranspiration and local heat mitigation |
| Ocean | Indirect: reduced nutrient runoff |
| Organic matter | Food residue collection, humification amount, avoided incineration, soil organic carbon |

### Cooling Credit candidates

- Avoided heat generation from incineration
- Increased soil organic carbon
- Improved soil water retention
- Land-surface temperature reduction
- Organic matter circulation recovery

## 12. Common Scoring Proposal

Each business model is scored from 0 to 5 for each of the four circulations.

```text
0 = No relation or worsens the circulation
1 = Indirect improvement
2 = Small-scale improvement
3 = Clear improvement
4 = Strong improvement confirmed by multiple indicators
5 = Core improvement capable of restarting regional circulation
```

Overall score:

```text
Circulation Recovery Score
= Water phase-transition score
+ Atmospheric circulation score
+ Ocean circulation score
+ Food and organic matter circulation score
```

Maximum score is 20. Models scoring 15 or above are high circulation recovery models; 10–14 are medium recovery models; 5–9 are supporting models; 4 or below require redesign.

## 13. Evaluation Principles

```text
1. Do not evaluate by CO₂ reduction alone
2. Include measured cooling effects
3. Do not separate soil, water, atmosphere, ocean, and organic matter
4. Prioritize avoided incineration and organic matter return
5. Measure short-term effects and long-term accumulated effects separately
6. Evaluate the connection between regional and planetary circulation
7. Exclude cooling measures that damage ecosystems
8. Include MRV feasibility in investment decisions
```

## 14. Conclusion

The eight business models are not merely revenue activities.

Each is an implementation unit for restoring planetary circulation.

Cooling cities restores water phase-transition and atmospheric circulation.

Humifying food loss restores organic matter circulation and soil water retention.

Regenerating forests restores evapotranspiration, carbon fixation, and water-source recharge.

Supporting ocean circulation restores heat, oxygen, nutrients, and phytoplankton.

MRV and Cooling Credit measure these recoveries, assign value to them, and guide capital toward circulation recovery.

Therefore, four-circulation recovery indicators are the implementation evaluation foundation for rewriting Civilization OS.
