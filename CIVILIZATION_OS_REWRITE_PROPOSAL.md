## 4. Simultaneous Recovery of the Four Planetary Circulations

### 4.1 Water Phase-Transition Circulation

Water moves, disperses, and buffers heat through evaporation, condensation, cloud formation, rainfall, freezing, and melting.

Recovering this circulation requires soil water retention, forest evapotranspiration, wetland restoration, urban water cycles, rainwater storage, reclaimed water use, water-retentive pavements, and mist cooling.

### 4.2 Atmospheric Circulation

Atmospheric circulation connects water vapor, clouds, rainfall, winds, updrafts, and heat transport.

Forest loss, urban heat accumulation, dryland expansion, and evapotranspiration decline alter the flow of water vapor and heat into the atmosphere.

Recovery requires evapotranspiration restoration, green infrastructure, urban cooling, wetland regeneration, and land-surface temperature reduction.

### 4.3 Ocean Circulation

Ocean circulation governs Earth’s largest heat capacity, carbon cycling, nutrient cycling, and oxygen cycling.

If heat remains in the surface layer, stratification increases and vertical circulation weakens. This affects phytoplankton, carbon fixation, and oxygen supply.

Recovery requires surface cooling, vertical circulation support, oxygen supply, nutrient cycling, and restoration of phytoplankton foundations.

### 4.4 Food and Organic Matter Circulation

Food circulation does not mean only human food supply.

It is the organic matter cycle linking plants, fallen leaves, food residues, waste, livestock manure, microorganisms, humus, soil, and crops.

If organic matter is incinerated or discarded instead of returning to soil, soil microorganisms, water retention, humus, carbon fixation, evapotranspiration, and food production all weaken.

Recovery requires food-loss humification, composting, returning leaves and pruned branches to soil, livestock manure management, microbial regeneration, and reduced incineration.
