## Overview

Humanity observed the rise of atmospheric CO₂ concentration and therefore came to understand global warming primarily as a problem of CO₂ emissions.

This view is partly correct. The relationship between greenhouse gases and temperature rise cannot be denied. The conclusion that human activity has caused global warming is also broadly correct.

However, the problem is that the internal structure of human activity has not been sufficiently decomposed.

Human activity is not only fossil-fuel combustion.

It also includes deforestation, mountain development, agricultural expansion, wetland loss, urbanization, road construction, weakening of soil microorganisms, decline of water retention, decline of evapotranspiration, monoculture, large-scale use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, nutrient runoff into rivers, ocean eutrophication, expansion of dead zones, and weakening of phytoplankton and ocean carbon cycling.

Despite this, climate action has long been strongly biased toward the single factor of CO₂ emissions.

---

## 1. The conclusion “human activity caused warming” is not specific enough

Modern climate discussions often conclude that global warming was caused by human activity.

This statement is not wrong, but it is too broad.

Human activity includes not only fossil-fuel combustion, but also land use, forest loss, soil degradation, urbanization, agricultural systems, water-cycle disconnection, and nutrient runoff into oceans.

Nevertheless, much of policy and market design has focused on CO₂ emission reduction, renewable energy, carbon credits, and emissions trading.

As a result, the historical causal structure of warming has not been sufficiently described.

---

## 2. CO₂ action is necessary, but it does not restore lost cooling functions

CO₂ reduction is necessary.

However, CO₂ reduction alone does not restore lost forests.

It does not restore damaged soil microorganisms.

It does not restore lost water retention.

It does not restore weakened evapotranspiration.

It does not restore disrupted cloud formation and rainfall cycles.

It does not restore eutrophicated oceans.

It does not restore weakened phytoplankton and ocean carbon cycling.

Therefore, even if CO₂ emissions are reduced, global warming cannot be sufficiently stopped unless Earth’s natural cooling functions themselves are restored.

---

## 3. What recent decades show

For more than a decade, the world has declared climate action.

Yet global CO₂ emissions have not sufficiently reversed, and global temperature rise has not stopped.

Recent years have seen repeated records in global heat, ocean heat, extreme weather, droughts, heavy rainfall, and wildfire risk.

This does not mean CO₂ is irrelevant.

On the contrary, CO₂ remains important.

But it means that treating CO₂ as the only causal target has been insufficient.

## 4. Redefinition by Master

Master’s definition reframes global warming as follows:

```text
Global warming is not caused by CO₂ emissions alone.
It is the result of population growth, land conversion, forest loss,
soil degradation, water-cycle disconnection,
weakening of water phase-transition processes,
decline of evapotranspiration, cloud formation, and rainfall cycles,
and weakening of ocean circulation and phytoplankton,
all of which reduce Earth’s natural cooling functions.
```

Therefore, the complete solution must consist of three layers:

```text
1. Emission reduction
2. Carbon fixation and CO₂ removal
3. Recovery of natural cooling feedbacks
```

CO₂ action is necessary.

But it is part of the complete solution, not the whole solution.

What is truly needed is emission reduction plus restoration of Earth’s cooling functions themselves.

This document connects to the following historical causal chain:

```text
Population growth after the Industrial Revolution
    ↓
Increased demand for food, housing, timber, and fuel
    ↓
Conversion of forests, grasslands, and wetlands
    ↓
Decline of carbon sinks, soil microorganisms, water retention, and evapotranspiration
    ↓
Historical warning from the 1930s Dust Bowl
    ↓
Chemical agriculture and monoculture after the 1950s
    ↓
Nutrient runoff, ocean eutrophication
    ↓
Expansion of hypoxic waters and dead zones
    ↓
Weakening of phytoplankton and ocean carbon cycling
    ↓
Loss of natural cooling feedbacks
```

This causal chain clarifies why CO₂-only climate action is not sufficient.

---

## 6. What this document claims and does not claim

This document does not reject CO₂ emission reduction.

It does not reject the science of greenhouse gases.

It argues that CO₂ action alone cannot restore the natural cooling functions lost through human activity.

In other words, CO₂ action is necessary, but not sufficient.

A complete solution to global warming requires emission reduction together with carbon fixation, soil regeneration, water-cycle restoration, forest and vegetation recovery, ocean circulation recovery, phytoplankton recovery, physical cooling, MRV, Cooling Credit, and Civilization OS.
