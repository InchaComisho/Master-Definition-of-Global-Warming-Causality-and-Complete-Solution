## Core supplement

The existing definition has emphasized the loss and weakening of water phase-transition processes as a central cause of global warming.

This is correct from the perspective of heat movement. Water moves, disperses, and buffers heat through evaporation, condensation, cloud formation, rainfall, freezing, and melting.

However, a complete definition must explicitly include circulation.

Earth’s cooling function does not depend only on heat transfer. It depends on the circulation of air, water, oceans, organic matter, life, and carbon.

## 1. Weakening of atmospheric circulation

The atmosphere is a circulation system that transports heat and water vapor.

Water vapor released from forests, wetlands, oceans, and soils moves through the atmosphere, forms clouds, and returns as rainfall.

When forest loss, urbanization, drying, surface heat accumulation, and evapotranspiration decline progress, the flow of water vapor and heat into the atmosphere changes.

As a result, cloud formation, rainfall distribution, seasonal winds, local winds, updrafts, and precipitation cycles become more likely to be disrupted.

Weakening of atmospheric circulation is not merely a change in wind. It is the spatial fragmentation of water phase transition and heat transport.

---

## 2. Weakening of ocean circulation

The ocean is the largest heat-capacity circulation system on Earth.

It absorbs heat and moves heat, carbon, nutrients, and oxygen through surface-deep exchange, currents, upwelling, and sinking.

If ocean circulation weakens, heat is more likely to remain in the surface layer.

If nutrient supply from deeper waters weakens, phytoplankton and ocean carbon cycling are also affected.

Therefore, ocean circulation recovery is not merely an ocean issue. It is a matter of planetary cooling, carbon fixation, oxygen supply, and life circulation.

---

## 3. Weakening of food and organic matter circulation

Food circulation does not mean only human food supply.

In this definition, food circulation refers to the organic matter cycle that connects plants, microorganisms, animals, humans, waste, fallen leaves, dead biomass, humus, soils, and crops.

In nature, fallen leaves, dead plants, animal waste, and dead organisms are decomposed by microorganisms, returned to soil, and used again to grow plants.

Through this circulation, soil fixes carbon, retains water, supports microorganisms, and enables plant evapotranspiration.

When incineration, chemical agriculture, monocropping, urbanization, and soil degradation prevent organic matter from returning to soil, soil loses its life circulation.

When soil loses organic matter circulation, carbon fixation, water retention, microorganisms, evapotranspiration, food production, and natural cooling functions weaken simultaneously.


## 6. Why a definition without circulation is incomplete

From the perspective of heat alone, the phrase “loss and weakening of water phase-transition processes” is correct.

However, Earth is not driven by heat alone.

The Earth cools, retains water, produces food, supplies oxygen, and supports life through the circulation of the atmosphere, oceans, organic matter, life, and carbon.

Therefore, climate action that does not include circulation cannot be called a complete solution.

A complete solution means not only reducing emissions, but restoring Earth’s atmospheric, oceanic, organic-matter, and life-circulation systems.
